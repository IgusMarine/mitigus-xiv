# Mitigus XIV — Product

register: product

## Product purpose
Mitigus XIV is a Windows-native tool that fixes FFXIV "double-weave" latency for a
PS5 player. The PC acts as the console's gateway, intercepts the game traffic, and
trims the animation-lock so skills stop "sticking" on high ping. This web panel is
the control surface: turn mitigation on/off, see at a glance that it's working, watch
network health, and follow a guide to connect the PS5.

## Users
The operator is a layperson, a friend running it on his Windows PC so his PS5
(Brazil to NA, ~350ms) plays smoother. Not technical. Often controls it from his
phone on the same Wi-Fi. Reads almost nothing; the panel must be obvious in two
seconds and, above all, reassuring: "is it on? is it working?"

## Brand / tone
Quietly confident; game-adjacent but never loud. A precision instrument for FFXIV,
not a gamer-RGB toy. Crystal / faceted motif (matches the app icon), ice-cyan accent.
Calm under high ping: it never alarms about the raw 350ms (that is physics), it
reassures about stability.

## Anti-references
- Generic SaaS dashboard: identical card grid, the hero-metric template.
- Gamer neon / RGB overload.
- Anything that reads as "AI made this".
- Alarming the user about numbers they cannot change (raw distance ping).

## Strategic principles
- One primary action dominates everything: the mitigation toggle.
- Prove it works with one satisfying truth: the animation-lock cut.
- A layperson on a phone understands the state in two seconds.
- Honesty in signal: alarm on jitter / instability, never on raw distance ping.
